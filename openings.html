Join Us!

The McVicker Lab is looking for people to join our team. We are an interdisciplinary group of individuals who strive to understand the genetics of chromatin and gene regulation. We welcome applications and inquiries from individuals from diverse experimental, computational and statistical backgrounds.

Our laboratory is currently using naturally occurring human genetic variation as a tool to understand how the genome specifies chromatin state and organization in immune cells. We are also interested in linking regulatory variation in immune cells to autoimmune and infectious disease risk. In much of our research we develop statistical and computational methods so that we can analyze and interpret data from genomics experiments such as ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq and Hi-C. If this type of research sounds exciting to you, contact us!

About the Salk Institute

The Salk Institute is an incredible and inspirational place to work! It was founded in 1960 by Jonas Salk, the developer of the Polio vaccine, and was designed by the renowned architect Louis Kahn. Today, the Salk Institute is an architectural landmark and is one of the top biological research institutes in the world.

The Salk Institute is located in beautiful La Jolla, California and is close to many other world-class research institutes including the University of California San Diego, the Scripps Research Insitute, the La Jolla Insitute for Allergy and Immunology, the Sanford Burnham Prebys Medical Discovery Institute and the J. Craig Venter Institute. Many biotechnology companies such as Illumina, Novartis and Human Longevity Inc. are also nearby.

Positions

Postdoctoral Reasearchers

We are recruiting motivated postdoctoral researchers to tackle cutting-edge research problems in human genetics, chromatin biology and immunology.

Applicants must have, or expect to receive, a Ph.D. in a relevant field such as genetics, molecular biology, immunology, computer science or statistics. Individuals from either computational or wet-lab backgrounds are encouraged to apply. Strong data analysis skills are considered a major plus.

Relevant skills (note: candidates are only expected to possess subset):

  • C/C++ or other compiled programming language
  • Python or other scripting language
  • R or other data analysis language
  • Proficiency in unix/linux
  • Knowledge of bioinformatics tools / software
  • Knowledge of statistics or machine learning
  • Knowledge of human genetics or population genetics
  • Experience in basic molecular biology techniques
  • Experience in next-generation sequencing experiments such as ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, Hi-C
  • Scientific writing and communication
