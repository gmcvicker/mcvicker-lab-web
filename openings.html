Join Us!

The McVicker Lab is looking for people to join our team. We are a highly interdisciplinary group of individuals who strive to understand the genetics of chromatin and gene regulation. We welcome applications and inquiries from individuals from diverse experimental, computational and statistical backgrounds.

Our laboratory is currently using naturally occurring human genetic variation as a tool to understand how the genome specifies chromatin state and organization in immune cells. We are also interested in linking regulatory variation in immune cells to autoimmune and infectious disease risk. In much of our research we develop statistical and computational methods so that we can analyze and interpret data from genomics experiments such as ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq and Hi-C. If this type of research sounds exciting to you, we encourage you to contact us!

About the Salk Institute

The Salk Institute was founded in 1960 by Jonas Salk, the developer of the Polio vaccine, and was designed by the renowned architect Louis Kahn. Today, the Salk Institute is regarded as both an architectural landmark and as one of the top institutes in the World for biological research.

The Salk Institute is situated in beatiful La Jolla, California and is close to many other world-class research institutes including the University of California San Diego, the Scripps Research Insitute, the La Jolla Insitute for Allergy and Immunology, the Sanford Burnham Prebys Medical Discovery Institute and the J. Craig Venter Institute. Many biotechnology companies such as Illumina, Novartis and Human Longevity Inc. are also nearby.
